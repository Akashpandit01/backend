1. What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)?
Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to computer systems designed to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence,
 such as learning, reasoning, problem-solving, perception, and decision-making. AI encompasses technologies like machine learning,
 natural language processing, and computer vision to enable machines to mimic cognitive functions.

2. What are Large Language Models (LLMs)? How do they work?
Large Language Models (LLMs) are advanced AI systems trained on massive datasets of text to understand and generate human-like language.
 They work using transformer architectures with billions of parameters, 
processing input through self-attention mechanisms to predict the next word or token based on context. 
Training involves unsupervised learning on internet-scale data, followed by fine-tuning for specific tasks.

3. What is the difference between Traditional AI and Generative AI?
Traditional AI focuses on rule-based systems, symbolic reasoning,
 and task-specific algorithms like expert systems or decision trees for narrow,
 predefined problems. Generative AI, a subset powered by deep learning models like LLMs or diffusion models, creates new content such as text, images, 
or code from learned patterns, emphasizing creativity and open-ended outputs rather than strict rule-following.

4. Explain the concept of "prompting" in the context of LLMs. Why is it important?
Prompting is crafting specific input text to guide an LLM's response, acting as instructions or examples to elicit desired outputs without retraining the model. 
It is important because it leverages the model's pre-trained knowledge efficiently, enables zero-shot or few-shot learning, and directly impacts accuracy, 
relevance, and creativity—poor prompts yield vague results, while effective ones unlock precise, context-aware generations.

5. What is the role of "tokens" in a language model, and how do they impact the output?
Tokens are the basic units of text (subwords, words, or characters) that LLMs process,
 converting input/output into numerical sequences via tokenizers. 
They impact output by defining context length limits (e.g., 128k tokens), influencing coherence in long responses, 
and affecting costs/computation—exceeding limits truncates text, while token choices shape fluency and precision.

6. What are some limitations or risks of using Generative AI models like ChatGPT?
Limitations include hallucinations (fabricating facts), bias from training data, lack of true understanding or reasoning, 
and context forgetting in long interactions. Risks involve misinformation spread, privacy breaches from data exposure,
 over-reliance reducing critical thinking, ethical concerns like job displacement, and potential misuse for phishing, deepfakes, or amplifying harmful stereotypes.
